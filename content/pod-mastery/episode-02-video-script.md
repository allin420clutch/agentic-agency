## SEGMENT 2: The 3-Question Test [2:30-4:15]

[TITLE CARD: "The 3-Question Test"]

[ON-SCREEN: Three question marks animate in, each turning into a checkmark as host explains]

Alright. You've found a niche that looks interesting. Before you invest hours designing, run it through **The 3-Question Test.**

**Question One: Is there a real community, not just a passing trend?**

Can you name Facebook groups, subreddits, or forums where this audience hangs out? Real communities have ongoing conversations, years of posts, and identifiable language. A passing trend has one viral moment and then silence.

**Question Two: Can I name FIVE specific products this audience would actually buy?**

Not vague ideas — actual product types. Mugs, shirts, totes, stickers, tank tops. If you can't list five quickly, you don't have a product line — you have a mood.

**Question Three: Can I write phrases this audience would proudly display?**

POD products sell through words as much as art. Can you imagine at least three phrases this group would wear or display with pride? If yes, you've got a design direction. If no, move on.

[ON-SCREEN: Checklist graphic with all three questions]

All three pass? You've got a niche worth pursuing.

One or two fail? Tighten up your niche or find a different one. This test saves you from wasting weeks on ideas that won't sell.

## SEGMENT 3: Red Flags and Green Lights [4:15-5:45]

[TITLE CARD: "Red Flags vs. Green Lights"]

[ON-SCREEN: Split screen with red X column and green checkmark column]

Let me make this really simple.

**Red flags — a niche is oversaturated if:**

- Every design looks the same
- Prices have dropped to $8-10 and everyone is competing at that level
- There's no room for your unique voice because one style dominates
- The audience can't explain what makes their group special

**Green lights — a niche has potential if:**

- The audience has inside jokes, special titles, or shared language
- Products are emotional — they make people feel seen, not just look nice
- You can find 3+ active communities they gather in
- There's a predictable seasonal spike you can plan around

[BACK TO HOST]

Look, I'm not saying avoid competitive niches entirely. But if you're going head-to-head with 10,000 other sellers, you better have a very specific angle. The research process helps you find that angle before you commit.
