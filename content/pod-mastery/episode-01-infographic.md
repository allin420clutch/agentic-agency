## 2. WHAT IS POD? — DEFINITION BLOCK

**Section Header:** `What is Print-on-Demand?`

**Body Text (2–3 sentences, beginner-friendly):**
> Print-on-Demand (POD) is a fulfillment method where products are **customized and created only after a customer places an order** — no inventory, no upfront printing costs, and no waste. You design, a third-party platform prints, packs, and ships the product directly to your customer under your brand.

**Visual anchor:** Consider a simple illustration of a screen with a design on the left and a shipped box on the right, connected by a dotted arrow.

## 10. COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

**Section Header:** `🚫 Avoid These Common POD Mistakes`

```
 1. ❌ Selling generic, low-effort designs
       → Niche down. Specific > cute.

 2. ❌ Ignoring shipping times in your pricing/description
       → Customers bail if shipping feels too long.

 3. ❌ Using low-resolution artwork
       → Always use 300 DPI minimum for print quality.

 4. ❌ Not testing multiple product types
       → A design that fails on a t-shirt may crush on a mug.

 5. ❌ Giving up after 2 weeks
       → Most successful POD stores took 3–6 months to gain traction.
```

**Visual treatment:** Numbered list with bold mistake text and a short explanation below. Use red/coral accent for the ❌ and green for corrections in the explanation text.

## 🎨 DESIGN GUIDELINES SUMMARY

- **Typography:** Bold, large headings (32–48pt). Body text at 14–18pt. Keep copy SHORT.
- **Icons:** Use flat, single-color icons throughout. Consistent stroke weight.
- **Color usage:** Indigo (`#4F46E5`) for primary elements/headers, Orange (`#F97316`) for accents/CTAs, Slate (`#64748B`) for muted text.
- **Whitespace:** Generous — don't cram sections. Each block should breathe.
- **Illustrations:** Simple, geometric style — avoid photorealistic images.
- **Data callouts:** Use large bold numbers, not small inline stats.
- **Flow diagram:** Use consistent box shapes and directional arrows throughout.
