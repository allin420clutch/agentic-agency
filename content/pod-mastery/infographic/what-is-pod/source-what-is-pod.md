## What is Print-on-Demand?

Print-on-demand (POD) is a business model where a third-party supplier prints, packs, and ships a product — like a T-shirt, hoodie, tote bag, or poster — **only AFTER a customer actually buys it**.

**Traditional E-commerce Model:**
Design → Buy Inventory → Store It → Ship It

**Print-on-Demand Model:**
Design → Customer Buys → We Print & Ship

You never touch the product. You never hold inventory. You don't even need a warehouse. The moment someone places an order on your store, that order gets sent directly to your POD partner — they print it, package it, and send it straight to your customer — usually within **3–7 business days**.

**You handle the creative side. They handle the physical side.**

---

## Step-by-Step Workflow

### Step 1 — You Create a Design
Using Canva, Photoshop, Illustrator — whatever you prefer. You upload it to your POD platform as a mockup. The key is understanding **print areas** and **file requirements**.

### Step 2 — You Connect a Store
You link your POD account to your Shopify store, Etsy shop, or standalone website. Products appear in your store with your design on them — priced with your profit margin on top.

### Step 3 — A Customer Places an Order
Your customer buys a product. You collect payment. The order is automatically routed to your POD partner.

### Step 4 — Printing & Fulfillment
Your POD partner — whether it's **Printful**, **Printify**, **Gooten**, or others — prints your design onto the product using either **DTG (direct-to-garment)** printing or sublimation, depending on the product type. They inspect it and pack it.

### Step 5 — Shipping & Customer Delivery
The product ships directly to your customer with your branding — or neutral packaging, depending on your settings. Tracking info is sent to both you and the buyer automatically.

### Step 6 — You Get Paid the Difference
Your store price minus the POD wholesale cost equals **your profit**. The customer pays. The supplier gets paid their cut automatically. You keep the remainder.

**No inventory. No shipping logistics. No packaging runs to USPS. Just design, sell, profit.**

---

## Why POD is Great for Beginners

- **Near-zero upfront cost** — You only pay when you make a sale
- **No inventory risk** — You will never be stuck with a warehouse full of unsold Tees
- **Easy to start** — You can launch a store in an afternoon
- **Built-in fulfillment** — The hard logistics part is handled for you
- **Scalable** — When you get busier, you do not need to hire more people or rent more space

---

## Key Stats & Facts

- **Fulfillment time**: 3–7 business days
- **Startup cost**: Near-zero (pay only when you make a sale)
- **Platforms mentioned**: Printful, Printify, Gooten
- **Printing methods**: DTG (direct-to-garment), sublimation
- **Store platforms**: Shopify, Etsy, standalone websites

---

## What to Know Before You Start

- POD is not a "get rich quick" scheme
- Profit margins can be thinner than expected once you factor in ad costs and platform fees
- Quality control is something you actively have to manage, especially when you're starting out
