## Section 1: What is Print-on-Demand?

**Key Concept**: POD is a business model where products are printed and shipped only AFTER a customer buys, eliminating inventory risk.

**Content**:
- Print-on-demand (POD) is a business model where a third-party supplier prints, packs, and ships a product (T-shirt, hoodie, tote bag, poster) ONLY after a customer buys it
- Traditional E-commerce: Design → Buy Inventory → Store It → Ship It
- POD flips this: Design → Customer Buys → We Print & Ship
- You never touch the product, never hold inventory, no warehouse needed
- Fulfillment time: 3–7 business days

## Section 2: The 6-Step POD Workflow

**Key Concept**: The complete POD supply chain flow from designer creation to customer delivery.

**Content**:

### Step 1 — Create a Design
- Use Canva, Photoshop, Illustrator
- Upload design to POD platform as mockup
- Key: understand print areas and file requirements

### Step 2 — Connect a Store
- Link POD account to Shopify, Etsy, or standalone website
- Products appear with your design, priced with your margin

### Step 3 — Customer Places Order
- Customer buys product
- You collect payment
- Order auto-routed to POD partner

### Step 4 — Printing & Fulfillment
- POD partner prints design (DTG or sublimation)
- Partners: Printful, Printify, Gooten
- Inspection and packing

### Step 5 — Shipping & Delivery
- Ships directly to customer
- Your branding or neutral packaging
- Tracking info to both buyer and seller

### Step 6 — You Get Paid
- Store price − POD wholesale cost = YOUR PROFIT
- Supplier gets cut automatically
- You keep the remainder

## Section 5: What to Know Before You Start

**Key Concept**: POD requires effort in design, has thinner margins than expected, and requires quality control.

**Content**:
- POD is NOT a "get rich quick" scheme
- Profit margins can be thinner once ad costs and platform fees are factored in
